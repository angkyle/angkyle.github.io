You walk into the cave.
It seems okay to stay for the night.
It's too dark out now to find food to eat, even though you're feeling pains of hunger.

Luckily, you find a rusty—but working—lighter and make a fire.

With an empty, aching stomach, you struggle to sleep.

You lie there in the night, with the scent of mold and mud lingering over you, a darkness pushing against you, as the sun kisses the sky farewell.
